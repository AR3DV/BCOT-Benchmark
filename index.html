Abstract

     Planar object tracking plays an important role in computer vision and related fields. While several benchmarks have been constructed for evaluating state-of-the-art algorithms, there is a lack of video which has really groundtruth. In this paper, we present a carefully designed planar object tracking benchmark containing 72 videos of 3 objects sampled in the randering environment. In particular, for each object, we shoot twelve videos involving various challenging factors, namely FreeHelix，FreeInplane, FreeOutplane2， FreeOutplane2， FreeTranslation，FreeZoom，RotationHelix， RotationInplane，RotationOutplane2，RotationOutplane2， RotationTranslation，RotationZoom. The ground truth is carefully annotated semi-manually to ensure the quality. Moreover, eleven state-of-the-art algorithms are evaluated on the benchmark using two evaluation metrics, with detailed analysis provided for the evaluation results. We expect the proposed benchmark to benefit future studies on planar object tracking.
